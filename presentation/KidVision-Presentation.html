📋 The Challenge We're Solving

Current State

  • Students rarely see their own progress data
  • Assessment results live in teacher gradebooks
  • Limited student engagement with learning goals
  • Data is numbers without context

What Research Shows

  • Effect size of 1.33 for self-reported grades (Hattie, 2023)
  • Self-assessment improves achievement by 0.5-0.7 effect size
  • Formative feedback doubles learning gains (Black & Wiliam)
  • Goal-setting effect size: 0.68
"When students become their own teachers, they exhibit the self-regulatory attributes that seem most desirable for learners."
— John Hattie, Visible Learning (2009)
📚 Research Sources:
• Hattie, J. (2023). Visible Learning: The Sequel - Meta-analysis of 2,100+ studies
• Black, P. & Wiliam, D. (1998). "Inside the Black Box" - King's College London
• Zimmerman, B.J. (2002). Self-Regulated Learning - Educational Psychologist

💡 Our Solution: KidVision

A student-facing dashboard that transforms assessment data into an engaging, gamified experience while maintaining complete district data compliance.

Visual Progress Tracking

Points-Based Motivation

OneDrive Integration

🔒 Data Security & District Compliance

KidVision uses Microsoft OneDrive - the same platform already approved and used by our district.

🏫

No External Databases

All student data stays within district-managed Microsoft 365 environment. No third-party servers.

👨‍�

Student's Own OneDrive

Each student's data is stored in THEIR OWN OneDrive account - completely isolated and private.

🔐

Microsoft Authentication

Students sign in with their existing school Microsoft accounts. No new passwords to manage.

📋

FERPA Compliant

Data stays within district control. No student information leaves the Microsoft 365 ecosystem.

✅ Why This Approach Was Chosen

After initial consideration of external database solutions, we pivoted to OneDrive to ensure 100% district data policy compliance while giving students ownership of entering and tracking their own progress.
